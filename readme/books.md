# Top 20 Books for Trading and Strategy

A curated reading list for systematic traders. Grouped by theme — read in order within each section.

---

## Foundation: Price Action & Market Structure

| # | Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets** | John Murphy | The bible of technical analysis. Every indicator, pattern, and intermarket concept. Start here. |
| 2 | **How to Make Money in Stocks** | William O'Neil | Inventor of CAN SLIM. Covers fundamental + technical stock selection. Practical, data-driven. |
| 3 | **Trading in the Zone** | Mark Douglas | The psychology book every trader must read. Explains why you sabotage profitable systems. |

---

## Trend Following & Momentum

| # | Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|------|--------|-------------|
| 4 | **Trend Following** | Michael Covel | Documents the track records of the Turtle traders. Proves that trend following works across markets. |
| 5 | **The Little Book of Trading** | Michael Covel | Condensed principles of trend following — beginner-friendly version. |
| 6 | **Following the Trend** | Andreas Clenow | Quantified trend-following on futures. Full strategy rules with backtest data. |
| 7 | **Stocks on the Move** | Andreas Clenow | Applies trend-following to equities (ETFs and stocks). Position sizing, ranking, rebalancing. |

---

## Mean Reversion & Quantitative Methods

| # | Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|------|--------|-------------|
| 8 | **Quantitative Trading** | Ernest Chan | How to build a systematic strategy from scratch. Covers Sharpe ratio, drawdown, backtesting bias. |
| 9 | **Algorithmic Trading** | Ernest Chan | Pairs trading, mean reversion, ETF arbitrage. More advanced than book #8. |
| 10 | **Short-Term Trading Strategies That Work** | Larry Connors & Cesar Alvarez | Empirically tested mean-reversion strategies — RSI(2), %b, Bollinger Bands. India-applicable. |

---

## Risk Management & Position Sizing

| # | Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|------|--------|-------------|
| 11 | **The New Market Wizards** | Jack Schwager | Interviews with top traders. Every one of them emphasises risk management above all else. |
| 12 | **Van Tharp's Definitive Guide to Position Sizing** | Van K. Tharp | The most complete book on position sizing strategies (fixed fractional, Kelly, volatility-based). |
| 13 | **Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom** | Van K. Tharp | System design, expectancy, R-multiples. Changes how you think about wins and losses. |

---

## Breakout, Pattern, and Momentum Strategies

| # | Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|------|--------|-------------|
| 14 | **How to Trade in Stocks** | Jesse Livermore | Livermore's own method. Pivot points, "lines of least resistance," and trading with the market. |
| 15 | **Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard** | Mark Minervini | VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern), SEPA methodology. Best stock selection book for growth stocks. |
| 16 | **Mastering the Trade** | John Carter | Squeeze momentum indicator, gap strategies, catalyst-based entries. Directly applicable to intraday. |

## Indian Markets & Derivatives

| # | Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|------|--------|-------------|
| 17 | **Option Volatility and Pricing** | Sheldon Natenberg | Core options theory — IV, Greeks, pricing models. Essential before trading any F&O strategy. |
| 18 | **The Options Playbook** | Brian Overby | Visual reference for every option strategy with profit/loss diagrams. Best practical F&O handbook. |
| 19 | **NSE Pathshala Resources** | NSE India (free) | Free NCFM workbooks on technical analysis, derivatives, and Indian regulations. Required reading for Indian traders. |

## Reading Order Recommendation

**Beginner path (books 1–3 → 8 → 11 → 13)**
Start with Murphy for concepts, O'Neil for stock selection, Douglas for psychology, Chan for quant thinking, Schwager for wisdom, Tharp for position sizing.

**Momentum trader path (books 15 → 7 → 16 → 6)**
Minervini for setups, Clenow for systematic execution, Carter for intraday, Following the Trend for the full system.

**Bot builder path (books 8 → 9 → 12 → 10 → 6)**
Chan #1 → Chan #2 → Tharp position sizing → Connors for signals → Clenow for full system.
